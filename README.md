# Reactistry

Reactistry is a factory-building and automation game focused on real chemical synthesis and process engineering.
The game combines the creativity and optimization of factory simulators with the logic of chemistry, creating an experience that is both educational and engaging.

In Reactistry, players solve increasingly complex production challenges by creating molecules through real chemical reactions.
Starting from basic raw materials, players must design automated production chains where compounds react together to form intermediate products, which can then be processed further into advanced molecules and materials.

The first version of the game focuses primarily on the stoichiometry of chemical reactions.
Players learn how to balance reactions correctly, manage reactants and products efficiently, and optimize chemical production systems.
Every process must be carefully planned to ensure the correct products are obtained while minimizing waste and handling byproducts appropriately.

Beyond simply producing molecules, players must build and optimize entire chemical factories:

- Automate production lines 
- Transport and distribute compounds efficiently 
- Reuse or dispose of secondary products 
- Optimize resource usage and reduce waste 
- Design scalable and logical industrial systems 

The game encourages analytical thinking, problem-solving, and systems engineering while introducing fundamental concepts of chemistry and chemical engineering in an interactive way. 

Future releases aim to significantly expand the depth and realism of the simulation. Planned features include:

- Economic systems with production costs and revenue 
- Advanced industrial equipment and apparatus 
- Reaction conditions such as temperature, pressure, catalysts, and solvents 
- More chemically accurate synthesis pathways 
- Real-world references from scientific literature 
- Educational facts and explanations about molecules and reactions 
- Realistic waste management and chemical disposal systems 

As the player progresses, tasks become increasingly difficult and require more advanced planning and optimization.
Many challenges may require several attempts before an efficient solution is found, encouraging experimentation and learning through iteration.

Reactistry is designed not only as an entertaining factory game, but also as a way to introduce players to the core ideas behind chemistry and chemical engineering.
The goal is to make scientific thinking accessible, rewarding, and fun while giving players a genuine understanding of how chemical production systems work.
